Code for Good

I was invited to participate in JP Morgan’s Code for Good Hack-a-thon. Through a virtual event with a team of five other members, we were challenged with developing a website for a non-profit organization that promotes young girls to participate in sports. We had 24 hours to create the webpage, message board, and database of users using python and html. This was a great exposure to working effectively in a team environment. Our team was awarded 1st place by the non-profit organization.

Hack-Q-Thon

This hackathon's challenge was to develop an application that utilized quantum technology. In under 30 hours I independently developed a web interface to input different stock portfolios. Then the backend used Qiskit's quantum optimization algorithms to perform portfolio optimization. To make the challenge more difficult, I decided to implement a 'fund of funds' optimization, meaning you were limited to investing index funds instead of individual stocks. I placed third in this competition.

During my senior year of high school, I won the US Congressional App Challenge for the 3rd District of Illinois. Although the nature of the app, a nostalgic water ring game, was simple in nature, I challenged myself by developing my own physics engine. This was my first major coding project. Utilizing hydrodynamic drag, I simulated the rings floating in water. I also factored in the gyroscope of the phone to determine how the rings would fall. It was shocking experience to be present my app to congressman in washington DC.
