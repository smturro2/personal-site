Code for Good

I was invited to participate in JP Morgan’s Code for Good Hack-a-thon. Through a virtual event with a team of five other members, we were challenged with developing a website for a not-for-profit organization that promotes young girls to participate in sports. We had 24 hours to create the webpage, message board, and database of users using python and html. Because this was a 24hour challenge, some of my teammates focused solely on completion of just their assignment. I and one other member took the lead to ensure the front end and back end worked together. This was definitely a lesson on how to work in a team environment. Our team was granted 1st place.

Hack-Q-Thon

This hackathon's challenge was to develop an application that utilized quantum technology. In under 30 hours I independently developed a web interface where you can input different stock portfolios. Then the backend would use Qiskit's quantum optimization algorithms to perform portfolio optimization. The challenge in this scenario is you can not invest in each stock independently, you must invest in the whole portfolio. Similar to how you would purchase CDOs. I placed third in this competition.

During my senior year of high school, I won the US Congressional App Challenge for the 3rd District of Illinois. Although the nature of the app, a nostalgic water ring game, was simple in nature, I challenged myself by developing my own physics engine. This was my first major coding project. Utilizing hydrodynamic drag, I simulated the rings floating in water. I also factored in the gyroscope of the phone to determine how the rings would fall. It was shocking experience to be present my app to congressman in washington DC.
